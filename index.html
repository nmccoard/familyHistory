Let's Build the Columbus Temple!

The Kirtland Ward is building a model of the Columbus Temple

Here is how you can help.

  1. Do a Family History Activity

  2. Tell us about by filling out the form

  3. The Temple Builders will add the bricks you earned to the model

Additional Information
  • The mode consists of 1,086 bricks.
  • You contribute a brick by participating in Family History activities
  • Every activity contributes one brick times the number of people the helped with it.
  • When you complete an activity, go to the "Contribute a Brick" section and submit your activity.
  • Each week, the submissions will be totaled and the contributed bricks will be added to the temple's construction.
  • Youth from the ward will be selected each week to be Temple Builders to help place the bricks
  • Check back every week to see the building progress!
  • Looking for ideas? Click on the "Activity Ideas" button or talk to Family History Consultant.

    Temple and Family History Activity Ideas

    Family Connections Experiment

    Visit the Family Connections Experiment where you will discover ready-made plans full of short, simple, and easy activities that go beyond traditional genealogical tasks.
    They have ideas for children, youth, adults, and families that will help increase and strengthen family connections and broaden your understanding of how you can engage in family history work.

    Indexing

    Log on to FamilySearch.org and start indexing. Consider setting a goal to index a certain number of names and/or batches. Need help? There are great tutorials on the FamilySearch site as well as helpful tips and examples for each indexing batch you work on. Or you can always reach out to one of your ward Temple and Family History Consultants to receive some one-on-one instructions and assistance.

    Four Generations

    Fill our your family tree to at least four generations. If any of those family members do not have their temple work done, reserve any temple ordinances that need to be completed and have a family member or ward member perform that work. As always, our ward Temple and Family History Consultants are ready to assist you!

    Recipe

    Find and cook a favorite family recipe. As you eat, talk about the story behind the recipe: where it came from and why it is part of your family story. If the recipe is from an ancestor, upload it to FamilySearch as a Memory

    Family Tradition

    Interview a grandparent, parent, uncle, aunt, or other close relative to discover how a tradition was started in your family. Consider creating a story about how this tradition started and share it in Family Tree.
    Here are some questions you can ask:
    — What is a memorable moment from your childhood?
    — What do you remember about where you grew up?
    — How did you meet your spouse?
    — What are some moments that impacted your life the most?

    BillionGraves

    Across the world, gravestones are deteriorating and erosion is erasing the precious details of our ancestors' lives. With BillionGraves, invaluable information recorded at their final resting place will still be available.
    How can you help? Grab your smartphone, download the free BillionGraves app to your device, and head out to the cemetery. It's as easy as taking pictures of each grave as you walk along. The app will capture the GPS location automatically and in just a short visit hundreds of records can be preserved.
